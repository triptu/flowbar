Get Flowbar

Free, open source, runs entirely on your Mac.

Download from GitHub
  1. Open the downloaded Flowbar.dmg and drag Flowbar into your Applications folder.
  2. Launch Flowbar. macOS will show a warning — this is normal for apps that aren't notarized (signing certificates cost money!).
  3. Open System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down, and click "Open Anyway". You only need to do this once.
  4. That's it! Double-tap Fn to open Flowbar from anywhere.
Why the security warning? Apple charges money for a developer certificate to skip this prompt. Flowbar is a free, open-source side project — so it's not notarized. But you can verify everything: the DMG is built automatically by GitHub Actions from the public source code. You can also build it yourself if you prefer.

Why you'll like it

It stays out of your way until you need it.

✎

Quick capture

Click the menu bar icon or double-tap Fn. Start typing. Your notes save automatically — no "save" button, no popups.

☑

All your tasks, one place

Scatter to-dos across any of your notes — Flowbar finds them all and shows them in a single list. Nothing falls through the cracks.

⏲

Focus and keep track

When you're juggling ten things, it's easy to lose track of what you were working on. Pick a task to focus on and stay anchored.

♢

Everything is Markdown

Plain text files you can open anywhere. No lock-in, no proprietary format — your notes are always yours.

⏱

Built-in stopwatch

Start a timer on any task. See how long things actually take — great for staying honest with your time and building better habits.

🔒

Private and offline

No account, no login, no cloud. Everything stays on your Mac. It just works, even without the internet.

Tip: If you use Obsidian, create a folder in your vault and point Flowbar to it. Quick access to your most important notes without opening Obsidian.
